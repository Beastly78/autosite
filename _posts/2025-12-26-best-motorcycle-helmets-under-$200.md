**Section 1: Safety First - What to Look for in a Motorcycle Helmet**

While helmets under $200 won't have all the bells and whistles of high-end models, it's essential to still ensure that your new lid meets the necessary safety standards. Look for:

* DOT, Snell, or ECE certification - these ensure the helmet meets rigorous impact and penetration tests
* A solid, sturdy shell construction
* Adequate ventilation and airflow to keep you cool and comfortable
* Adjustable fit systems for a snug, secure feel
* Visor or shield to protect your eyes from wind and debris

**Section 4: Choosing the Right Fit for Comfort**

A well-fitting helmet is crucial to prevent chafing, discomfort, and even accidents. Here are some top tips for ensuring the ideal fit:

* Measure your head to identify your correct size (use the circumference of your head, not the crown)
* Experiment with different helmet styles and shapes to find the one that suits you best
* Optimize the fit with adjustable features like chin straps, cheek pads, and internal head liners
* Consider a helmet with a softer inner lining for added comfort and reduced fatigue
